## Purpose of this page

A use-case page converts visitors who are solution-aware but not yet convinced Karte fits their specific situation. Each entry answers: "Is this for someone like me?" The format — user, job, example, CTA — mirrors the Jobs-to-Be-Done framework and can be rendered as a card grid, accordion, or comparison table on the site.

---

## Use Case 1 — The Indie Hacker

**User:** Solo founder / indie hacker building in public. Active on Twitter/X and Hacker News. Gets daily DMs asking about their stack, revenue, and whether they're open to partnerships or acquisition chats.

**Job:** Filter inbound at scale without hiring or ignoring people.

> "I ship fast and post publicly. That means I get 10–20 DMs a week asking the same things: what's your MRR, are you bootstrapped, can I invest, will you collab? I was either ignoring good leads or wasting an hour a day copy-pasting answers."

**Example:** Vishnu built his profile on Karte and fed it his revenue milestones, stack, current projects, and a short FAQ on partnership criteria. Visitors who land from his tweet bio now get answers instantly via Chat. His inbox dropped by 60%. The Encyclopedia surface tells his full builder story — tools, pivots, lessons — so serious collaborators arrive informed, not exploratory.

**CTA:** Build your indie profile — answer DMs without opening them → `karte.cc/create`

## Use Case 2 — The Freelance Consultant

**User:** Freelance designer, engineer, or fractional operator with a public presence. Rates on request, availability unclear, project criteria unstated. Spends 30–60 minutes a week qualifying leads who are a poor fit.

**Job:** Qualify inbound before the first call, with zero effort.

> "My old link-in-bio had a 'Work with me' link that went to a Calendly. I'd get bookings from people who couldn't afford my rate, weren't in my niche, or wanted something I don't do. Every conversation started from scratch."

**Example:** Priya set up her Karte profile with her rate range, project criteria ("minimum 3-month engagements, product companies only"), current availability status, and a FAQ built from the last 50 DMs she'd received. Now her Chat surface pre-qualifies leads: it tells them her rate, explains her criteria, and asks about their project before they ever reach her. The contacts who book a call arrive already knowing what to expect. Her close rate on discovery calls went from 20% to 55%.

**CTA:** Set your rates once. Let Karte say them for you → `karte.cc/create`

## Use Case 3 — The Creator with a Newsletter

**User:** Newsletter writer or content creator with 2k–50k subscribers and a public profile. Gets questions on Twitter, Instagram DMs, and email: "how do I get started writing?", "what tool do you use?", "can I sponsor you?"

**Job:** Scale audience engagement without burning out on repetitive messages.

> "I write about building a media business. That means hundreds of people want to know the same ten things: my equipment, my process, my sponsor rates, how I grew my list. I was spending more time answering process questions than actually writing."

**Example:** Mara built a Karte profile and loaded her tool stack, sponsorship kit (rates, reach, lead time), writing process, and a "most asked" FAQ with 15 entries. She added a link in every newsletter footer: "Questions? My profile knows everything." The Newspaper surface auto-generates a weekly headline summary of her recent posts — shareable and updated automatically. Her DM volume dropped 40%; her most engaged readers became power users of the Encyclopedia, which tells her full creator story.

**CTA:** Let your profile answer your audience — free on Karte → `karte.cc/create`

## Use Case 4 — The Open-Source Maintainer

**User:** Developer who maintains a popular open-source library or tool. Gets GitHub issues, Twitter DMs, and Discord messages with the same questions: "does this support X?", "is there a roadmap?", "can I contribute?", "are you looking for funding?"

**Job:** Reduce repeated maintainer burden and direct contributors to the right starting point.

> "Maintaining an open-source project is rewarding but the questions are relentless. Half the issues opened are really questions that belong in the README — except the README is already 4,000 words. I needed something that would just *answer* people."

**Example:** Arjun linked his Karte profile in his GitHub README and pinned it in his Discord. He fed it his project FAQ, contribution guidelines, roadmap (public milestones only), and a personal note on what kinds of sponsorships he accepts. New contributors now arrive already knowing where to start. Issues dropped 30%. The Roast surface became a running joke in the community — every new release prompted someone to share the roast, driving 200+ new profile visitors per launch.

**CTA:** Give your project a profile that answers for you → `karte.cc/create`

## Use Case 5 — The Career Switcher / Job Seeker

**User:** Developer, designer, or PM actively job searching or making a career pivot. Has a LinkedIn profile but it doesn't tell a story — just titles and dates. Wants to stand out to recruiters and hiring managers without writing a cover letter for every application.

**Job:** Make a strong first impression and pre-answer recruiter questions, 24/7.

> "I was switching from backend to product. My resume didn't explain the 'why' — it just showed a series of engineering roles. I needed something that told my story: why I'm making the move, what I've shipped, what kind of company I want to join."

**Example:** Tariq built a Karte profile as his 'career page'. He loaded it with his story (the pivot narrative, the product work he'd done inside engineering roles), his top three project case studies, and a FAQ for recruiters ("Are you open to remote?", "What's your notice period?", "What size company?"). He added a link in his LinkedIn bio: "Get the full picture at karte.cc/tariq." Recruiters who clicked it arrived at a conversation-ready profile. Tariq got three recruiter interviews from cold outreach — all mentioned reading his profile before reaching out.

**CTA:** Tell your story once. Let every recruiter experience it → `karte.cc/create`

## Page layout recommendation

Render as a 5-card grid with:
- Card header: persona name + icon (e.g. "The Indie Hacker", "The Freelance Consultant")
- Card body: 1-line job statement + 2-sentence example result
- Card footer: CTA button linking to `/create`
- Optional: tab or toggle to expand the full story

Each card should link to the corresponding long-form blog post (Cluster C from the glossary plan) for SEO depth. The short card version lives on the use-case landing page; the long version lives as an indexed blog post.

---

## Voice and tone notes

- Use second-person ("you") only in CTAs; use third-person named personas in examples to create social proof by proxy.
- Keep the job statement to one sentence: active verb + object + constraint. ("Filter inbound at scale without hiring.")
- Keep the example story under 60 words on the card; expand only in the blog post version.
- Avoid jargon: "AI profile" is fine; "LLM-powered conversational bio" is not.
- The CTA copy should complete the job: not "Sign up" but "Set your rates once. Let Karte say them for you."
