## 3-Second Value Score: 6 / 10

A cold visitor with 3 seconds reads "Stop answering the same DMs" and "Your link in bio already knows what you'd say." They get the pain and a vague product shape — but not the category.

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Problem clarity | 8/10 | DM fatigue hook is crisp and resonant for creators/builders |
| Product clarity | 4/10 | "Link in bio that already knows" implies AI but never says it; category is undefined in above-the-fold copy |
| Audience fit | 6/10 | "People who get inbound" is relatable but narrow — excludes people who *want* inbound |
| Urgency / why now | 4/10 | "Claim your name" implies scarcity but the trust line buries "Free" below the CTA |
| **Overall** | **6/10** | Good pain hook, weak product definition |

**Biggest gap:** The word "AI" never appears above the fold. The four surfaces (Chat, Encyclopedia, Newspaper, Roast) — the actual differentiator — are invisible until a deep scroll. Someone landing from a tweet doesn't know this is AI-powered or interactive.

---

## Structural issues

1. **AI is invisible above the fold.** The product is an "AI link-in-bio" but neither word appears in H1 or the sub. Visitors who land on the page cold — especially from non-tech audiences — won't know.

2. **Four surfaces are below the fold.** Chat / Encyclopedia / Newspaper / Roast is the entire value proposition but you have to scroll past two sections to see it. The hero needs a one-liner that previews this.

3. **Target audience is too narrow in the eyebrow.** "For people who get inbound" excludes people who are building their profile to *start* getting inbound. A subtler reframe: "For builders, creators, and anyone who hates inbox management."

4. **No social proof above the fold.** The trust line is excellent but numbers/testimonials/user count are absent. Even "3,000 profiles built" would change the credibility calculus.

5. **Demo → CTA gap.** The chat demo is the best part of the hero. There's no nudge from the demo to sign up. A single line under the demo ("Ready to set up yours? → Claim your name") could lift conversion.

## Copy rewrite suggestions

### Option A — AI-first positioning (recommended for cold traffic)

```
Eyebrow: Your AI-powered link in bio

H1: The profile that
    answers for you.

Sub: Feed it your rates, story, and stack.
     Visitors get instant answers in your voice —
     four ways to discover who you are.

CTA: Build yours free →    See a live profile ↗
Trust: No card · 60-second import from Linktree or Beacons
```

**Why:** Names the category (AI link-in-bio), previews the 4-surface idea, keeps the live demo meaningful.

---

### Option B — Pain-first (keep current H1, tighten sub)

```
Eyebrow: For builders and creators who get inbound

H1: Stop answering
    the same DMs.

Sub: Karte is an AI link-in-bio. Give it your memory —
     rates, projects, FAQs — and visitors get answers
     in your voice before they hit your inbox.

CTA: Claim your name — Free →    See it live ↗
Trust: No card · 60-second import from Linktree or Beacons
```

**Why:** Preserves the strong H1, clarifies what Karte is in the first breath of the sub, avoids jargon.

---

### Option C — Output-first (for virality / social ads)

```
H1: One link.
    Four ways to meet you.

Sub: Chat answers your DMs. Encyclopedia tells your story.
     Newspaper headlines your week. Roast makes it shareable.
     All from the same memory you maintain once.

CTA: Start free →    See a live example ↗
```

**Why:** Names all four surfaces above the fold, optimised for people who see a surface first (screenshot, tweet) and land wanting to know what the whole thing does.
